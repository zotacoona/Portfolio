Vision

Design Philosophy

Life Centered Design

A philosophy that views design not just through the lens of human needs, but considers the long-term impact on all forms of life and future generations. Moving beyond basic sustainability to enrich social, environmental, and emotional systems.

The Object

Every object is approached not merely as a decorative tool, but as a holistic experience defined by its visual, tactile, and functional qualities. The object becomes memorable and desirable through carefully developed colors, materials, and textures.

The Experience

Products are built around user interactions, spanning the first visual impression, ergonomics, durability, and emotional connection over time. This ensures the product consistently fulfills user expectations and enhances daily life.

Crafting a Compelling Story

Storytelling is the bridge between raw engineering and human emotion. Working closely with the Industrial Design team, a CMF scheme and palette are built to align material choices with overall product goals and business objectives.

Open conversations and shared perspective help CMF and ID teams make sure that color and finish decisions boost the product's visual appeal while also supporting its strategic launch objectives in the market.

Research & Strategy

Color & Material Initiatives

Color & Material Standardization

Establishing military and aerospace level color systems ensures precision across multiple manufacturing plants. Working with standards like **AMS STD 595A** (U.S. color standard for government and military procurement) allows for strict control of color boundaries under varying light sources.

Our data-driven color match workflows employ spectrophotometer data collection, assessing L*a*b* values to establish acceptable delta-E ranges for production anodization on machined aluminum.

  • Spectrophotometer calibration & L*a*b* tolerance mapping
  • AMS STD 595A color alignment for government procurement
  • Cosmetic anodization thickness and reflectivity controls

Spectrophotometer Color Match

Executing exact color evaluation and digital data collection. Capturing spectral curves to prevent metamerism issues across different light conditions (D65, TL84, A).

Anodization Assessment

Testing cosmetic anodization on machined premium aluminum substrates. Balancing chemical bath time, temperature, and dye concentration for a repeatable metallic finish.

CMF Prototyping

Translating concepts into tangible prototypes with actual part geometries. Crucial to verify paint flow, texture density, and prevent handoff issues with factories.

Engineering the Details

CMF Development Journey

Additive Manufacturing

Structural Aluminum 3D Printing

3D printing with aluminum allows for complex organic structures and internal geometries that are impossible to machine or cast. During this initiative, we analyzed structural support layouts, laser sintering heat distributions, and post-processing steps.

We verified surface roughness and executed design fit checks of sintered parts to confirm cosmetic validity for high-end technology enclosures.

  • Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) processing
  • Post-sintering surface finishing (bead blast, polish)
  • Support structure optimization to prevent warping
Substrate Engineering

Aluminum Die-Casting

For high-volume structural components, aluminum die-casting provides a cost-effective, high-strength solution. However, cosmetic out-of-tool substrates often present defects like swirl marks, flow lines, and porosity.

Our work focuses on perfecting the tool gating system and applying specialized surface primers and powder coated finishes to completely mask casting blemishes.

  • Out-of-tool substrate defect characterization
  • High-pressure die-cast tool gate optimization
  • Cosmetic coverage verification via paint thickness
Surface Treatment

Powder Coating & Masking

Powder coating offers excellent resistance to scratching, impact, and chemical wear. Implementing high-yield powder coating requires optimized masking fixtures to protect electrical grounding contacts and fine threading.

We designed custom silicone masking plugs and engineered line-hanging racks to prevent uneven spray thickness and drip defects at high line speeds.

  • Custom high-temperature silicone masking fixtures
  • Electrostatic powder spray gun angle tuning
  • Thermal curing cycle oven monitoring
Material Selection

Substrate Material Matrix

Creating a systematic substrate and surface treatment matrix forms the core of our technical documentation. We investigate how different grades of aluminum (5000, 6000, 7000 series), magnesium alloys, and polycarbonates interact with anodization, paint, or plating.

This matrix allows engineering teams to balance mechanical stiffness, cosmetic appearance, weight, and unit cost.

  • Cross-material surface roughness testing
  • Adhesion rating (Cross-hatch tape test ASTM D3359)
  • Corrosion resistance comparison (Salt spray testing)
Validation & QA

CMF Process & Design of Experiments

Optic CMF & Material Experiments

Advanced devices, such as virtual and mixed reality headsets, require sophisticated optic CMF characterization. We design custom "test vehicles" to analyze visible light transmission, haze, and reflectivity of glass and polymers.

Using **Design of Experiments (DOE)**, we systematically evaluate factors like coating thickness, baking temperature, and pigment concentration. This ensures consistent IR (infrared) emissions and texture paint specs across production lots.

  • Visible light transmission & reflectivity spectroscopy
  • IR emissive texture paint formulation
  • Systematic DOE factor analysis to isolate defects

Cosmetic Quality Defect Severity Matrix

Quality Level Assessment Defect Severity Engineering Build Use Key Remarks & Specs
Level 1 (OK) Pass No defect Yes Meets all ID/CMF cosmetic expectations. Pass through cosmetic spec.
Level 2 (NG) Fail Minor Waiver upon Approval Minor scratches or micro-particles inside non-critical viewing areas (Class B/C surfaces).
Level 3 (NG) Fail Moderate Waiver upon Approval Visible flow marks, paint runs, or structural anodization discoloration on Class A surfaces.
Level 4 (NG) Fail Critical No Peeling coat, bubbling paint, cracked substrate, or out-of-tolerance dimensions. Rejection required.
